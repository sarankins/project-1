About Honolulu

Honolulu is a vibrant city offering a mix of cultural heritage, sandy beaches , and natural beauty. Visitors can explore historic sites, try delicious local food, and enjoy the beauty of waikīkī. It's no wonder it's one of the favorite places to visit!

Top Attractions in Honolulu

  • Waikīkī Beach

    Waikīkī beach is known for it's long stretch of white sand and turquoise blue water, everyone enjoys this paradise for swimming, surfing, snorkeling, and more. From Hilton Hawaiian Village on the west side to Kapi'olani Park on the east, Waikiki has a beautiful tropical climate with many activities and events for all ages.

  • Pearl Harbor

    Step into history at Pearl Harbor, the site of the infamous 1941 attack that changed the course of World War II. Visit the USS Arizona Memorial, pay tribute to the lives lost, and explore fascinating museums and historic ships like the USS Missouri. Whether you're a history buff or seeking a reflective experience, Pearl Harbor offers a powerful and educational look at one of America's most significant moments in time.

  • Turtle Snorkling at Hanauma Bay

    Discover vibrant marine life at Hanauma Bay, one of Honolulu’s top snorkeling spots. This protected marine embayment offers crystal-clear waters, colorful coral reefs, and an abundance of tropical fish. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced snorkeler, Hanauma Bay provides an unforgettable underwater adventure in a stunning natural setting. Don’t forget to bring your camera—this is paradise beneath the waves! This is a popular snorking destination where you can see green sea turtles. They float above the reef as reef fish swim around them, cleaning their shells. Whether you're in the water or on a boat, you'll have an amazing view of these beautiful turtles.

  • Hawaiian Lū'au

    A Hawaiian luau is a traditional feast that celebrates Hawaiian culture through a combination of delicious food, vibrant music, and captivating hula dancing, showcasing the warmth and hospitality of the islands. The centerpiece of a luau is usually the "kalua pig," a whole pig slow-cooked in an underground oven. This is a family friendly event celebrating the land and traditions.

Interesting Facts About Honolulu

  • Population: 350,000
  • Language: English and Hawaiian
  • Average Weather: Honolulu has a tropical wet and dry climate with wet winters
  • Geography: As the capital of Hawaii, Honolulu is about 10 miles long on the south corner of Oahu island.
